Vasamuseet

THE FAMOUS VASA

The Vasa is one of Sweden's iconic symbols, it represented Sweden's rise to global power during the 16th Century. Upon its completion, Vasa was one of the strongest and most decorated ships in the world, however with the amount of weaponry it carried (mainly bronze cannons), it became unstable and eventually sunk less than 2000 meters out into sea. Upon its retreaval, scientists and archeologists have preserved the majority of the ship's hull and its many trinkets found within it- it is now permenantly housed in Vasamuseet, Stockholm, and is one of scandinavia's biggest attractions.

The Vasa was a ship famous for showing Swedish craftsmanship, strength and design to the entire world. Vasamuseet (Vasa Museum) showcases it in all its glory, taking you through its history, from how it’s been built to the people who have helped to build it. One of the museum’s most recent attractions “Vasa’s Women” shows the conditions women had to face during the time of the ship’s build and emphasises on the community aspect of how locals came together to complete the vessel.

The museum is suitable for everyone: it’s a great day out, educational for both children and adults. There’s a restaurant located above the ground floor if ever you’re feeling hungry or needing some time to relax. With it offering a vast amount of traditional Swedish dishes, you just have to try!

Vasamuseet is in Djurgården, Stockholm.

The opening times and admissions can be found on their website: Vasamuseet

Enjoy your visit!
